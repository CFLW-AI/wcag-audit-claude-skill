Oklahoma Learning Innovation Summit

Beyond AI-Proofing

Pedagogy and Design in the Age of Intelligence

Michael Hanegan

Part One

Working Assumptions

Four premises that frame everything that follows.

Assumption 01

Nothing is Immune to Generative AI

No discipline, no assignment type, no assessment model is untouched. The sooner we accept this, the sooner we can design for it.

Assumption 02

The Fundamental Crisis is Pedagogy + Design

Not "Academic Integrity." When we frame the problem around cheating, we miss the deeper transformation happening beneath our feet.

Assumption 03

Hostile Postures Accelerate Unwanted Behaviors

Banning, policing, and surveillance do not prevent misuse — they make it so much worse. The arms race approach has already failed.

Assumption 04

These Are the Earliest Days of the Future of Knowledge Consumption and Production

What we're experiencing now is the floor, not the ceiling.

Part Two

Why We Are Panicking

GenAI isn't the source of the crisis — it's the spotlight illuminating problems that already existed.

The Diagnostic

What the Spotlight Reveals

  • A simplistic view of skill acquisition — we confuse direct performance with nested capability
  • Academia ignored Goodhart's Law — when the measure becomes the target, it ceases to be a good measure, and introduces unintended behavioral change
  • Faculty spend little or no time on knowledge consumption and production in their own fields
  • No AI policy — other than FORWARD — is truly useful or even enforceable

Part Three

The Challenge + Opportunity

The Reality

Three Hard Truths

  • There is so much we do not know — our traditional approaches to knowledge consumption and production no longer work in this reality
  • We cannot simply harden our infrastructure and design — the walls won't hold
  • We should not accept GenAI as both the destroyer and savior of learning

Part Four

Experiments at the Frontier

Five areas of active experimentation in the future of learning and work.

1

Experiment

Transformation of Information Engagement

  • Nested skills, workflows, and tools — not isolated prompts, but integrated systems of thinking
  • Platforms like NotebookLM, Claude, and Notion as orchestration layers for knowledge work
2

Experiment

Pedagogy of Transparency

  • Meaningful and applicable design that demonstrates real value to students
  • An invitation to success rather than enforcement of policy — designing experiences students want to engage with authentically
3

Experiment

Curation of the Analog

  • Not as prevention, but as investment — analog experiences become more valuable, not less
  • Modeling metacognition and learning metabolism — teaching students how they learn, not just what to learn
4

Experiment

Multimodal Rendering of Results

  • The unique cognitive work of a multimodal iteration approach — thinking across formats, not just within one
  • An honest admission that some elements of traditional knowledge production are approaching obsolescence
5

Experiment

Rapid Acquisition + Assessment of Emerging Best Practice

  • AI-enhanced discovery — using the tools to find and evaluate what's working in your own field
  • GenAI tools as screening and engagement mechanisms for staying current at the frontier

The Path Forward

Stop AI-Proofing.
Start Designing.
